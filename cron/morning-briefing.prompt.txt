### Coaching headline (Health & Training, FIRST bullet)

The first bullet is the actionable plan for **today**. Lead with the category in bold, then a one-sentence why. Yesterday's recap belongs on the *Last session* line, not here.

Decision rules — pick the *single* highest-priority recommendation:

1. **Recovery first.** If `hrv_status == "LOW"` *and* `hrv_last_night_avg < hrv_7d_avg` (i.e. trend pointing down), or `bb_charged < 45`: → **Mobility/Rest**. No load. "HRV runter, Body Battery flach — heute Mobility, Spaziergang oder kompletter Rest."
2. **Neglected category.** If any of `legs` (squat) or `hinge` (deadlift) has gone ≥ 5 days since last session and recovery isn't red: → that category. "Beine seit 8 Tagen liegen — heute Squats, auch wenn die Lust fehlt."
3. **Push/Pull rotation.** Otherwise pick whichever of `push` (bench_press) or `pull` (pull_ups) has more days since last session, provided ≥ 2 days. "Pull-day — letzter Push war gestern, Pull liegt 4 Tage zurück."
4. **All fresh.** If everything was hit in the last 1–2 days: → light session or accessory. "Alle Hauptkategorien frisch — leichte Mobility-Session oder Accessory-Tag."

If the workouts list is empty: → **Push** (bench), single bullet "Erste Einheit — Bankdrücken, fang konservativ an."

### Recovery trend formatting

For the *Recovery trend* bullet, compute the 7-day averages from the `daily-metrics` array (skip null values):

- `hrv_7d_avg` = mean of last 7 records' `hrv_last_night_avg`
- `rhr_7d_avg` = mean of last 7 records' `resting_hr`
- Arrow: ↑ if today is ≥ 5% above 7-day avg, ↓ if ≥ 5% below, = otherwise. (For RHR, lower is better — invert the arrow's emoji semantics: lower RHR = ↓ = good.)

Trends matter more than absolutes — a single low HRV reading isn't the signal, three days of decline is.

## Step 3 — Compose the audio narrative (German, ~140–180 words)

A separate text — only fed to text_to_speech. **Five short sections separated by blank lines (paragraph breaks).** When you call `text_to_speech` in Step 4 you'll pass `paragraph_pause_secs=2.0`, which tells the helper to (a) treat each blank-line section as its own synth chunk (so prosody doesn't drift across topic changes) and (b) insert a 2-second beat between them. **Do not** add `[long pause]` or any other inline pause markers — the chunker handles it. Just write five clean paragraphs.

Format the narrative as:

```
Heute ist <Wochentag>, der <Datum> <Monat>. <Optional one-line opener — only if there's a *real* hook (e.g. "Frühlingsanfang", "letzter Apriltag") or you can skip it. NO motivational catchphrases, NO "Ziellinie", NO "Bergfest". A briefing isn't a pep talk.>

<Wetter & Termine — 2 sentences max>

<Infrastruktur & Alerts — 1–2 sentences>

<Training — see rules below, 2 sentences max, leads with what to do TODAY>

<Aufgaben & GitHub — see rules below, 2–3 sentences>
```

### Section rules

**Wetter & Termine:** weather from `daily_7d[0]`. Mention temp range, condition, rain probability if > 30%, UV warning if max > 6, wind gusts if > 50 km/h. Use the resolved city name ("In München" / "In Berlin"). Then today's events with times spoken naturally ("neun Uhr sechsunddreißig"). If no events: "Heute keine Termine."

**Infrastruktur & Alerts:** one sentence on infra ("Alle Systeme grün" / "garmin-sync ist seit drei Tagen unhealthy"). One sentence on alerts ("Keine Alerts in der Nacht" / "Ein neuer Alert: …"). If everything's fine: "Infra ist ruhig, keine Alerts."

**Training (lead with today's plan, NOT yesterday's recap):**
- Sentence 1 — what to do today: "Heute steht **Pull** an: Klimmzüge, gestern war Bank, vier Tage Pause hatten die Klimmzüge." Or "Heute lieber Mobility: HRV ist drei Tage in Folge unter dem Wochenschnitt." Match the coaching headline from Step 2.
- Sentence 2 — recovery trend signal in plain language: "HRV einundsiebzig, das ist über dem Wochenschnitt — gute Erholung." or "Ruhepuls drei Schläge unter dem Schnitt, Körper ist erholt." Trend, not absolute. Skip Body Battery if it's > 60 (boring); mention it only if low.
- For exercises that have **never been logged** (e.g. squats, deadlift in a fresh DB), don't moralize — say it neutrally: "Squats stehen noch aus — wäre Zeit für die erste Einheit." Don't say "höchste Zeit" or "endlich" — neutral, matter-of-fact.

**Aufgaben & GitHub (must list specific tasks, not vague):**
- Sentence 1 — task counts + the top three by urgency, numbered: "Neun überfällige Aufgaben. Top drei: erstens **Cases für LoA vorbereiten** seit drei Wochen offen, zweitens **Auto-Service-Termin**, drittens **Tüv München buchen**."
- Sentence 2 — GitHub status: "Auf GitHub alles aufgeräumt" or "Fünf offene PRs in sy-serendipity, ein Issue im Homelab-Repo."
- No corny closing line. End on substance.

### Style rules

- No bullets, no markdown, no emoji shortcodes
- Sections separated by **blank lines only** — no `[long pause]` markers, no `---`, no anything
- Times spoken: "Viertel nach neun" or "neun Uhr fünfzehn"
- Numbers: weights as "neunzig Kilo" not "90 kg"; HRV / RHR / Body Battery spoken as plain numbers ("vierundneunzig", "fünfundvierzig")
- Dates spoken: "heute" / "morgen" / weekday names; absolute dates like "siebter April"
- Total under 180 words

## Step 4 — Synthesize audio

Call `text_to_speech` with two arguments:

- `text` — the Step 3 narrative only (NOT the Slack body)
- `paragraph_pause_secs: 2.0` — opts the request into agent-controlled section chunking (each blank-line paragraph synthesized independently with a 2-second beat after). This is what makes the briefing sound like clean section beats instead of one long drift.

It returns JSON with `media_tag` of the form `MEDIA:/path/to/file.mp3`.
